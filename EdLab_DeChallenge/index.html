Unuseless Usage of Science

» EdLab Design Challenge     » ORBIT TRANING     » Kassar Gallery
Intro

Unuseless Usages of Science is an exhibition with a combination of the project ORBIT TRAINING, the knowledge behind it and its inspiration, some early science magazine covers in the same space. These usages of science are not really functional, maybe even ridiculous at current time, however not useless at all and showed the other side of science as a type of art and future encouragement.

Experience

In Kasser Gallery, people first see and are attracted by an unexpected moving shadow of a planet shape display board, which is an illusion created by the main project ORBIT TRAINING via real time shape capturing. Getting closer, audiences are introduced to the project's concept which tells the story of human beings on Earth by outer space science, through the interactive fake shadow experience as well as the description on display board. People are invited to move freely in the space, in group or by themselves.

On the pedestals, selected early science magazine covers are displayed where viewers may find the source of some modern technologies and art. And in the end of the exhibition, imagine table is designed for audiences to complete the connection between a science term and an everyday item. Their answer could be a short sentence or a simple sketch. They can also write down a new question for other audiences.



Besides

Unuseless Usages of Science explores how interactive expereince and interdisciplinary thinking could be brought to audiences and benefit future learning and education.





ORBIT TRAINING

ORBIT TRAINING is a real-time interactive installation designed for future life in outer space to help people get better understanding of how orbit works so that they can keep themselves in the orbit of those they care about. The project creates an illusion by capturing people’s real shadow in front of the light, then making it always moving from right to left and generating the new shadow. The closer people get to the wall, the faster the shadows moves in the invisible orbit. The sound of white noise is also generated based on the distance.

Science Behind

An object in motion will stay in motion unless something pushes or pulls on it. This statement is called Newton's first law of motion. Without gravity, an Earth-orbiting satellite would go off into space along a straight line. With gravity, it is pulled back toward Earth. An object's momentum and the force of gravity have to be balanced for an orbit to happen. If the forward momentum of one object is too great, it will speed past and not enter into orbit. If momentum is too small, the object will be pulled down and crash. Actually, all human beings have gravity that we never noticed. We also change our speed to keep the orbit from each other. In ORBIT TRAINING, audiences become an Earth without even noticing it at first. The moon changes its shape because of the shadow, changes its speed because of the audience’s existence in the space.





Design For Audiences at Teacher's College and Kassar Gallery

Thinking Process

Kasser Gallery is a small but also one of the most active space in the library. The challenge of exhibiting in this non-traditional gallery environment also provides the chance of having more audiences quickly past the space. Most people in this space are:
» Waiting for the elevator     » Entering/Leaving to the library     » Heading to the café     » Sitting on the stairs making phone calls
All won’t stay for a long time.

I was seeking the answer to the questions:
» How can the exhibition quickly attract people’s attention and getting closer?     » What info/knowledge could be delivered in a short period of time?     » And most importantly what could education in fragmented time bring other than just the knowledge itself?

Solutions

ORBIT TRAINING is chosen because the biggest part of it is empty space to be filled by people which is a perfect match with Kassar Gallery. The white noise sound could be set to mute when required for the library. At the same time, the installation provides an interactive experience for science learning and an innovative way of artistic story telling using science knowledge.

Adjustments and additional designs are made to create an integrated experience. The planet shape display board is placed in the middle of the main installation to give an initial shadow and evoke people's curiosity. Descriptions and relative knowledge are displayed there so that audiences stop in front of the light to read and notice their own shape on the projection. They will also see the shadow speed changes when walking to the pedestals in the back of the room.

Learning always takes time so the exhibition never try to have audiences get lots of info or master a skill. The project Orbit Training, early magazine covers, and Imagine Table together show them that science is fun and beautiful and interdisciplinary thinking or even imagination. The attempt is made to inspire audiences' own learning process and future innovations for education.
