Online Book Display - Classics of the Harlem Renaissance

» Work @ Teachers College Columbia University     » Role in the project: Designer & Front End Development

» 2020 April     » @ Gottesman Libraries Website

Classics of the Harlem Renaissance is a special digital book display in celebration of the 100th anniversary of the Harlem Renaissance. It seeks to help visitors understand not only African American history and culture, but the Harlem Renaissance as a significant step towards the American ideal of freedom and opportunity.

While the scheduled onsite display and events were postponed in light of the COVID-19 outbreak, interactions and jazz music effects are designed on the online platform to make more interesting and attractive user experience.

Main Tools

Web Design | Frond End Development | Illustration

Concept Development


Platform Study
- A new subpage under the Gotteman Libraries website

    Pros
  • Easy and quick access for users and library visitors
  • Most users and visitors are already familiar with the website structure
    Cons
  • Compared to onsite physical exhibits, visitors are less patient and less likely to come back to the webpage. How to get users to check all the books and contents is a challenge
  • The whole design has to fit into the existed website structure, including visual styles and interactions.


Solution
I got inspired when doing the research on Harlem Renaissance, where jazz music is big part of the cultural movement. I decided to use music as the main concept and add sound effects as part of the interaction. Jazz musical instruments become book stands, and when a book is "flipped", the corresponding instrument sound will be played. The design seeks to bring some fun and freedom to create music for users in the difficult time, while showing the topic from multiple dimensions.
